UID Devices

2015 - Interaction Design - 1-month student project

A pop-up exhibition for UID'15. UID Yearly events are often home to an anxious lot. With this in mind, and in order to engage the public in a pop-up exhibition, we designed a set of "UID Devices" that could be interacted with throughout the event.

The users

People attending UID'15. Especially students presenting thesis projects, or people eager to network, that might be feeling anxious and stage-frightened.

The needs

Having had presentation classes (and classes with presentations) throughout the year, we were able to spot common afflictions to a person speaking publicly.

Ideation

We chose three questions that seemed the most common: How do I look? Am I being heard? Are my hands shaking? If people could have an answer to these questions, perhaps they'd be a little less anxious before presenting or talking to someone.

Concept

REASSURING,
FUNNY +
SPARK INTERACTION

The main attributes for the devices "personality" were guided by the needs of the public: anxious users would benefit from soothing and reassuring answers; funny answers could also help to break the ice. At the same time, these answers should spark interaction between atendess.

This attributes are reached by the design through the semantics of the given answers: they're reassuring because "low markings" are delivered with a kind tone; also, the hyperbolic nature of the "top markings" are non-sensical and fun. The gamification of the ranking system sparks interaction between atendees, as they feel compelled to "challenge" each other.

NO. 1 - Voice Inspector

Built with a microhpone inside, it ranks the volume of the voice of a person talking in front of it. The minimum value - "lovely whisper" - aims to be kind instead of accusing.

NO. 2 - Looks Inspector

With a presence detector and a mirror, it tells the person standing in front of it wheter he or she look OK. In fact, it only randomly blinks green or yellow (67% and 33% odds), but the red was kept there so people can feel reassured they didn't get it.

NO. 3 - Stress inspector

A game where the user tries to bring the key from one end of the rail to the other without touching, and can check his or her score along the markings, to compare and challenge others.
